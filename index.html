My Services

Paper Crafts

Paper crafts involve working with various types of paper to create beautiful, functional, and artistic items.

  • Scrapbooking: Capturing memories through photos, embellishments, and decorative designs.
  • Card Making: Designing personalized greeting cards for special occasions, using stamping, embossing, and cutting techniques.
  • Origami: The art of folding paper into intricate shapes such as animals, flowers, and geometric designs.
  • Paper Mâché: Creating sculptures and objects by layering strips of paper soaked in glue or paste.
  • Decoupage: Decorating objects like boxes, trays, or furniture with paper cutouts, then sealing them with a layer of varnish or glue

Knitting & Crochet

Knitting and crochet are textile crafts that involve creating fabric from yarn to make clothing, accessories, or home decor.

  • Knitting Scarves, Hats, & Sweaters: Using knitting needles to create cozy and fashionable pieces like scarves, hats, sweaters, and more.
  • Crochet Amigurumi: The Japanese art of crocheting small, stuffed animals or characters, often used as toys or decorations.
  • Blankets & Throws: Creating soft, warm blankets using various knitting or crochet techniques like cables or granny squares.
  • Crochet Mandalas & Wall Hangings: Making intricate, circular designs to hang as wall art or decorative pieces for the home.

Sewing & Fabric Crafts

Sewing and fabric crafts involve working with textiles to create both functional and artistic projects.

  • Quilting: Stitching together layers of fabric to create blankets, wall hangings, or table runners, often with unique patterns and designs.
  • Embroidery & Cross-Stitch: Adding decorative stitches to fabric, creating intricate designs and patterns on clothing, linens, or home decor.
  • Appliqué: Sewing pieces of fabric onto a larger piece to create designs, often used for clothing or home items like pillowcases.
  • Fabric Painting: Using special paints to decorate fabric items like t-shirts, bags, or home decor, creating custom designs.
  • DIY Clothing & Accessories: Making your own fashion items like dresses, skirts, and bags or revamping old clothing with your personal style.

Jewelry Making

Jewelry making is a craft where various materials like beads, metal, and stones are used to create custom jewelry pieces.

  • Beadwork: Using beads and threads to create intricate jewelry pieces such as necklaces, bracelets, and earrings.
  • Wire Wrapping: The art of wrapping wire around stones, crystals, or beads to create unique jewelry designs.
  • Resin Jewelry: Embedding small objects, like flowers or glitter, in clear resin to create custom jewelry pieces like pendants and earrings.
  • Metal Stamping: Using stamps and hammers to imprint designs, names, or phrases on metal pieces, commonly for personalized jewelry.
  • Leather Jewelry: Crafting jewelry from leather pieces, such as bracelets or earrings, often incorporating beads, charms, or metal components.

Woodworking

Woodworking includes creating items from wood, from simple decorative pieces to intricate furniture.

  • Carving: Shaping wood using tools like knives or gouges to create sculptures, detailed designs, or functional objects.
  • Woodburning (Pyrography): Burning designs into wood using a heated tool to create decorative images or patterns on items like coasters, spoons, and signs.
  • Furniture Restoration: Repairing and refurbishing old furniture to give it new life, often by sanding, staining, and reupholstering.
  • DIY Home Decor: Crafting wood-based home decor, such as picture frames, shelves, or signs, to add a personalized touch to any space.
  • Woodworking for Kids: Simple and fun projects for children, like building birdhouses or small toys, using safe tools and easy techniques.

Painting & Drawing

Painting and drawing are traditional art forms that let you express creativity through various mediums and techniques.

  • Polymer Clay Jewelry: Sculpting small, colorful designs from polymer clay to create earrings, necklaces, and bracelets.
  • Pottery and Ceramic Sculpture: Molding clay by hand or using a pottery wheel to create pottery, vases, bowls, or sculptural pieces.
  • Air-Dry Clay Crafts: Using non-bake clay to create projects that dry naturally without the need for a kiln, such as ornaments or small sculptures.
  • Clay Coasters & Bowls: Crafting functional home decor items like coasters or bowls from clay, which can be personalized with colors or textures.
  • Pottery Painting: Adding your artistic touch to ready-made pottery pieces by painting or glazing them to create custom home decor.

Paper Flower Bouquet

Create a beautiful, everlasting paper flower bouquet by cutting and assembling petals from colored paper. You can use tissue paper for soft, delicate blooms or cardstock for sturdier, more structured flowers. Arrange the flowers into a bouquet and wrap the stems with floral wire or sticks, then tape them with green floral tape to give the bouquet a realistic look. These paper flowers make a perfect gift, home decor, or wedding centerpiece that will never wilt.

Colored paper (tissue paper, crepe paper, or cardstock), Scissors, Glue or tape, Floral wire or wooden sticks, Green floral tape

Upcycled Denim Tote Bag

Repurpose an old pair of denim jeans into a stylish and functional tote bag. Cut the legs of the jeans to the desired size for the body of the bag, then sew the edges together to form the main compartment. Use the waistband or the jean's belt loops to create straps for the bag. You can also add pockets, embellishments, or fabric paint for a personalized touch. This upcycled project is perfect for those looking to refresh their wardrobe while being eco-friendly by turning old clothing into something new.

Old denim jeans or fabric, Sewing machine, Fabric glue (optional), Scissors, Measuring tape, Needle and thread
